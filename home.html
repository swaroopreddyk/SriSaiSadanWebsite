SRI SHIRDI SAI BABA & DATTATREYA INCARNATE

Guru Brahma - Guru Vishnu Guru Devo Maheshwaraha - Guru Sakshath Parabramha Tasmay Sri Guravayana maha Tasmay Sri Sai Guravayana maha Tasmay Sri Datta Guravayana maha

The culture of Guru has been prevalent in sacred India since centuries. Our mother land has given birth for many sadhgurus and one prominent among them is Sri Shirdi Sai Baba. Sri Saibaba lived in Shirdi (in Maharastra) continuously for a period of 60 years before he took his mahasamadhi in 1918. No one knew the exact birth place, date and parents of Saibaba.At the age of sixteen, he was seen in Shirdi under a neem tree practicing hard penance not minding the heat and cold. He kicked out maya and had gained complete proficiency at this young age. People were astonished seeing this young lad practicing hard penance.

Sai's sermony

Saburi

'Saburi'; means patience and perseverance. Saburi is a quality needed throughout the path to reach the goal. This quality must be ingrained in a seeker from day one, least he looses his stride and leaves the path half way

Sai's preaching

Purity

For Sai Baba it was not the purity of the body but inner purity that mattered. No amount of physical and external cleansing would serve any purpose if the man remained impure in mind and heart. Therefore, Baba cautioned His devotees not to make austerity as an end itself, lest they should indulge in physical mortification.

2002

April 21, 2002: Sri Ramanavami Day - Chanting of "Om Sai, Sri Sai, Jai Jai Sai" was conducted through the day to sanctify the place April 25, 2002: Foundation stone was laid and Pooja was performed by the devotees collectively March 10, 2003: Dwarakamayi Baba's photo has been installed. From this day, poojas began in the temple on day-to-day basis.


2003

A May 12, 2003: Marble idol of Saibaba was brought from Jaipur. Pooja was performed to the idol at Shirdi. A three day event was organised in the temple premises to install the idol under the gracious blessings and supervision of His Excellence 'Sri Darga Swamy' Executive Officer of Sri Saibaba Sansthan - Shirdi, Sri.B.R.Wakchaure paid a visit to the temple. In his presence the Palanquin procession began in the temple. From this day onwards, every Thursday evening and on important festival days, the palki seva is conducted in the temple between 7:30 pm to 9:30 pm.

In the month of August 2003, Sai Satsang programme began in the mandir. Sri Allu bhaskar Reddy is conducting this programme in the temple since then on every Sunday evening. Lectures on Sri Saibaba, Sri Dattatreya and on other religious matters are given to enlighten the devotees February 25, 2005: Sri Chandrabhanu Satpathiji from Delhi visited the temple and delivered his valuable teachings on Saibaba and other Spiritual matters.
